Purple Carrot Recipes: Showcasing the Blue Transformation

These recipes are designed to showcase the fascinating color transformation of purple carrots. Each recipe includes tips on how to achieve the most vibrant blue colors or how to preserve the original purple hue.

Blue Carrot and Ginger Soup

Preparation: 15 minutes Cooking: 25 minutes Serves: 4

This clear soup beautifully showcases the blue transformation of purple carrots while the ginger adds a warming flavor that complements the carrots perfectly.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb (450g) purple carrots, thinly sliced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, minced
  • 1 small onion, finely diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda (to enhance blue color)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Fresh herbs for garnish (chives or parsley)

Method:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add onions and sauté until translucent, about 3-4 minutes.
  2. Add garlic and ginger, cook for another minute until fragrant.
  3. Pour in vegetable broth and bring to a temperature of exactly 185°F (85°C) using a thermometer.
  4. Add the baking soda and stir to dissolve.
  5. Add the sliced purple carrots and maintain the temperature between 180-185°F for 3 minutes.
  6. Watch as the carrots transform from purple to a vibrant blue color.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Serve in clear bowls to showcase the beautiful blue color, garnished with fresh herbs.

Color Tip: For the most vibrant blue, make sure your broth is slightly alkaline by adding the baking soda. Keep the temperature controlled - too hot and the color will be less vibrant.

Purple and Blue Carrot Salad

Preparation: 20 minutes Cooking: 5 minutes Serves: 4

This dramatic salad showcases both the original purple and transformed blue colors of purple carrots for a stunning visual effect.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 lb (225g) purple carrots, julienned
  • 1/2 lb (225g) purple carrots, thinly sliced for cooking
  • 2 cups mixed salad greens
  • 1/4 cup toasted walnuts, chopped
  • 1/4 cup feta cheese, crumbled
  • 1/4 red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda (for cooking water)
  • Dressing:
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon white wine vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon honey
  • 1/2 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Method:

  1. Set aside the julienned purple carrots - these will remain raw and purple.
  2. Bring a pot of water to 183°F (84°C) and add the baking soda.
  3. Add the thinly sliced purple carrots to the water and cook for exactly 2 minutes.
  4. Remove the now-blue carrot slices and cool them quickly in ice water to stop the cooking.
  5. Whisk together all dressing ingredients until well combined.
  6. In a large bowl, arrange the salad greens, raw purple julienned carrots, cooked blue carrot slices, red onion, walnuts, and feta cheese.
  7. Drizzle with the dressing just before serving.

Presentation Tip: Arrange the raw purple and cooked blue carrots in alternating patterns for the most dramatic visual effect. The contrast between the two colors is stunning!

Blue Carrot Pickles

Preparation: 15 minutes Cooking: 5 minutes Pickling: 24 hours

These vibrant blue pickled carrots make a beautiful addition to charcuterie boards, sandwiches, or as a colorful side dish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb (450g) purple carrots, cut into sticks
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 cup white vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda (for pre-cooking)
  • 2 cloves garlic, lightly crushed
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon black peppercorns
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 sprig fresh dill

Method:

  1. First, prepare the carrots: Bring a pot of water to exactly 183°F (84°C) and add the baking soda.
  2. Add the carrot sticks and cook for exactly 2 minutes until they turn blue.
  3. Remove and drain the blue carrots.
  4. In a separate pot, combine water, vinegar, sugar, and salt. Bring to a boil and stir until sugar and salt dissolve. Remove from heat.
  5. Place the blue carrot sticks in a clean jar along with garlic, mustard seeds, peppercorns, bay leaf, and dill.
  6. Pour the hot pickling liquid over the carrots, ensuring they are completely covered.
  7. Allow to cool to room temperature, then seal and refrigerate for at least 24 hours before serving.
  8. The pickles will keep for up to 2 weeks in the refrigerator.

Color Tip: The acidic pickling solution would normally turn the carrots back to purple, but by pre-cooking them in alkaline water, we create a more stable blue color that persists even in the acidic environment.
